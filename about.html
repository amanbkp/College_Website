About G.P. Bhagalpur

Government Polytechnic, Bhagalpur was established as Bhagalpur School of Engineering with aim of providing quality higher education in the field of Engineering & Technology on par with National Standards. It persistently seeks and adopts innovative methods to improve the quality of higher education on a consistent basis.

It is spread in an area of nearly 50 acres with four lush green campuses namely Main Campus, Kanchangarh Campus, ShreeBhawan Campus and Lakshmi BhawanCampus, not far from the bank of the River Ganga at Barari. It is situated at a distance of nearly 9 Km East to Bhagalpur Railway Station and 20 Km North to Naugachhia Railway Station.

The Institution was established on 31st May 1955 with its name Bhagalpur School of Engineering under the Department of Science and Technology, Govt. of Bihar which was later rechristened to its present name. Initially, the Institution was started with Diploma in Civil Engineering with 30 intake capacity, which was later increased to 60.

Our Mission

To provide student with comprehensive knowledge of Principles of Engineering with a multi-disciplinary approach.

To create an intellectual stimulating environment for Research, Scholarship, Creativity, Innovation and Job oriented professional activities.

To foster relationship with other institutes of Learning and Research, Alumni and Industries in order to contribute to National Development.

Our Vision

To lead quality higher education towards inclusive excellence through Institutional Transformation.

Courses We Offer

Computer Science

Covering topics related to computation, programming languages, program design, computer hardware, and software.

Electronics & Communication

Deals with electronic networks and devices, electromagnetic fields, computer fundamentals, and control systems.

Civil

Deals with the design, construction, and maintenance of the physical and naturally built environment.

Agricultural

Deals with the design of farm machinery, farm structures, drainage, water supply, and irrigation.

Textile

Deals with materials like cotton, fibers, and yarns used in clothing products and accessories.

Electrical

Deals with the study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism.
